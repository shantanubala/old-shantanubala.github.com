Statistical Formulas for Programmers

May 20th, 2013

Being able to apply statistics is like having a secret superpower.

Where most people see averages, you see confidence intervals.

When someone says “7 is greater than 5,” you declare that the data-generating processes are, in fact, indistinguishable.

In a cacophony of noise, you hear a cry for help.

Evan Miller has written an excellent resource detailing the numerous statistical formulas that are useful for programmers.

The resource describes the formulas and their applications, and is an excellent resource for getting a better understanding of data.
