The Attraction of Fan Fiction

Apr 11th, 2013

I just finished re-reading all of Eliezer Yudkowsky’s Harry Potter fanfic - Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality. After reading it again, I have a new appreciation for what Eliezer is attempting.

Writing fan fiction based on a series as popular as Harry Potter allows Eliezer to construct a new world from existing parts. Instead of having to spend enormous amounts of time establishing the histories and personalities of his characters, he can focus only on the parts that are different from the original work. It is through these differences that Eliezer is able to explain seemingly-complex concepts involving Bayesian Rationality, quantum mechanics, cognitive biases, and psychology.

The references to science seem forced at times, but the underlying story is gripping in a way that is very different from J.K. Rowling’s writing. Eliezer has elevated the art of fan fiction to a different level - instead of just constructing a story that extends the original, he constructs a story that transcends it.

Give it a shot. It’s worth reading.
