How to Actually Change Your Mind

May 4th, 2013

One of the most valuable things I have ever read is the LessWrong sequence How To Actually Change Your Mind.

I recommend it without hesitation to anyone who hasn’t already read it.

Here are some gems:

When you see people insisting that no amount of money whatsoever is worth a single human life, and then driving an extra mile to save $10; or when you see people insisting that no amount of money is worth a decrement of health, and then choosing the cheapest health insurance available; then you don’t think that their protestations reveal some deep truth about incommensurable utilities.

In the West, there is a saying: “The squaky wheel gets the grease.” In Japan, the corresponding saying runs: “The nail that sticks up gets hammered down.”

I believe there was a lawsuit where someone alleged that the draft lottery was unfair, because the slips with names on them were not being mixed thoroughly enough; and the judge replied, “To whom is it unfair?”

“If we cannot learn to take joy in the merely real, our lives will be empty indeed.”

The entire sequence is very long, but also worth reading through. It is the kind of thing that changes the way you think, and it includes numerous interesting footnotes and citations that provide an endless rabbit hole of thought-provoking content.
