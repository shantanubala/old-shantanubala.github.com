The Personal MBA

May 5th, 2013

The Personal MBA by Josh Kaufman is probably one of the best business books I’ve read. I usually don’t enjoy reading business books, but Kaufman does an excellent job of being precise and concise in his explanations of terminology and strategy.

In short, a business is a repeatable process that:

  1. creates or provides something of value that
  2. other people want or need
  3. at a price they’re willing to pay, in a way that
  4. satisfies the purchaser’s needs and expectations and
  5. provides the business sufficient revenue to make it worthwhile for the owners to continue operation.

As Kaufman says, everything else is a hobby.

Although Derek Sivers’ review of the book is a very good summary, the book itself is a must-read for anyone who wants to start a business.

A few key takeaways:

  • Test everything. Any assumption that is untested should be questioned unless there is external evidence (e.g. science) that validates the assumption.
  • Business is all about creating value and extracting a portion of the value that is created.
  • There are two things your business does: things that provide value and waste.
  • We are inherently biased towards the things we create. External validation is crucial.
  • Don’t delay a launch in search of perfection, but iterate towards it over time.
  • Measure the lifetime value of a customer and the cost of acquiring a customer through different channels. Once these variables are measured, and the cost of acquiring a customer is less than the lifetime value, profits will be easy to increase.
  • Test prices. Higher prices are usually not as bad as you may think.
